FemtoNet Explorer Virtual Reality Visualization of Quantum Femtography

The FemtoNet Explorer allows learners to "follow an electron" as it travels inside a particle accelerator. They watch the reaction step-by-step as the electron causes the proton target to emit photons that are detected, slowly forming a picture of the proton's structure.

The use of VR in the visualization is both important and novel. The novelty attracts learners who would otherwise ignore articles, movies, or animations. VR is an important medium because it grounds learners in a physical world and intuitively allows them to grasp the reality of particles instead of experiences a representational disconnect in the way learners do with screen media.

It was developed as part of a grant at the University of Virginia Physics Department in conjunction with Jefferson Lab and the School of Data Science. It functions as public outreach for an ongoing grant connected to a new particle accelerator. This is the alpha version. It was originally designed for the Oculus Quest, but desktop, SteamVR, iOS, and Android versions are planned
