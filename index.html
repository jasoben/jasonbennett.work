Exploring Electromagnetism via a Papercraft Maker Project, Augmented Reality Videos, Unity Simulations, and 3D Animation

The Papercraft DC Motor is a learning module for middle and high schools that combines die-cutting, electronics, AR videos, animations, and a Unity Simulation to explore electromagnetism. The goal was to create a module on electromagnetism and motors that doesn't require students or teachers to purchase $10-20 kits for each student. The papercraft motor can be built for $.50 / student, and teaches students functional building skills instead of the "plastic snap together" method of most kits.

It was tested successfully in local middle schools.

See videos, animations, download Unity program, and more

Flowerdew Hundred 3D Visualization of Harris Matrix

The Flowerdew Hundred Unity Sim is a WebGL widget that connects to a Drupal Database, allowing students to explore real data from an archeological dig and visualize it in relation to models and dirt strata. It is a novel way to explore the Harris Matrix-- a common tool in archeology

It is used by undergraduate students at the University of Virginia and is available via CC license to any interested party.

The Drupal -> Unity bridge was built by BitSource.

Visit and use the tool.

FemtoNet Explorer Virtual Reality Visualization of Quantum Femtography

The FemtoNet Explorer allows learners to "follow an electron" as it travels inside a particle accelerator. They watch the reaction step-by-step as the electron causes the proton target to emit photons that are detected, slowly forming a picture of the proton's structure.

The use of VR in the visualization is both important and novel. The novelty attracts learners who would otherwise ignore articles, movies, or animations. VR is an important medium because it grounds learners in a physical world and intuitively allows them to grasp the reality of particles instead of experiences a representational disconnect in the way learners do with screen media.

It was developed as part of a grant at the University of Virginia Physics Department in conjunction with Jefferson Lab and the School of Data Science. It functions as public outreach for an ongoing grant connected to a new particle accelerator. This is the alpha version.
