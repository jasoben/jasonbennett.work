Welcome

Thanks for visiting my site. While my day job is working with makerspaces and AR/VR at the University of Virginia, I have been curious about using 3D development tools to explore ideas since doing my masters work in education.

I am trained as an Instructional Designer (M.Ed 2015), and self-taught as a Unity Developer (practicing since 2014). I imagine “Immersive Learning” broadly as a way of thinking about the learner’s window into ideas; in non-immersive digital pedagogies the learner interacts with ideas through a small rectangular portal, i.e. a screen. Immersive Learning begins when the window disapears or, to put it another way, the learner steps through the portal into the world behind the screen.

Extended Reality (XR) technologies come with some baggage: mainly the “neat” factor of something relatively new and untested, which means lots of breathless sales pitches from self-proclaimed ed-tech oracles. The goal of my work is to stop, take a breath, and reflect on what makes for truly novel, effective immersive learning.

Services

I offer two main skills for any project: Instructional Design and 3D Development in Unity.

Instructional Design

I am trained in Instructional Design at the Masters level (M.Ed. University of Virginia 2015). I use the skills every workday in my day job, helping faculty imagine and realize novel and meaningful ways for learning in higher ed.

In my freelance work I focus primarily on applying instructional design methods to immersive learning through extended reality (XR) technologies. These methods typically include performing needs assessments, writing design documents, crafting learning goals, and assessing how well the project achieves those goals.

I both enjoy and believe in the model where the instructional designer is also the developer; fortunately I do both!

3D Development in Unity

I am a self-taught Unity developer (practicing since 2014). I use it frequently in my work, both for my day job, where I lead the Unity Community of Practice, and for freelance work. I also make games in my spare time.

Due to my training as an educator, I would say my greatest strength as a coder is I write C# code that is intelligible and instructive to my future self / others.
