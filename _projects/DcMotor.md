The Papercraft DC Motor is a learning module for middle and high schools that combines die-cutting, electronics, AR, and VR to explore electromagnetism. The goal was to create a module on electromagnetism and motors that doesn't require students or teachers to purchase $10-20 kits for each student. The papercraft motor can be built for $.50 / student, and teaches students functional building skills instead of the "plastic snap together" method of most kits.

It was tested successfully in local middle schools. Teachers interested in using the module should contact me via the link at the top.

# VR / Desktop Sim

<img src="/images/projects/dcmotor/screenshot-sim.png"/>

A Unity Sim that allows students to easily change parameters of the motor and see the effects. It also allows them to turn the electromagnetic fields on and off, while also slowing time, so they can "watch" a simulated papercraft motor work with added visualizations of electromagnetic forces.
