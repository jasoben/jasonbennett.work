Thanks for visiting my site. While my [day job](https://news.virginia.edu/content/meet-makers-theyre-turning-ideas-reality-all-over-grounds) is working with makerspaces and AR/VR at the University of Virginia, I have been curious about using 3D development tools to explore ideas since doing my masters work in education. 

I am trained as an Instructional Designer (M.Ed 2015), and self-taught as a Unity Developer (practicing since 2014). I imagine "Immersive Learning" broadly as a way of thinking about the learner's window into ideas; in non-immersive digital pedagogies the learner interacts with ideas through a small rectangular portal, i.e. a screen. Immersive Learning begins when the window disapears or, to put it another way, the learner steps through the portal into the world behind the screen. 

Extended Reality (XR) technologies come with some baggage: mainly the "neat" factor of something relatively new and untested, which means lots of breathless sales pitches from self-proclaimed ed-tech oracles. The goal of my work is to stop, take a breath, and reflect on what makes for truly novel, effective immersive learning.
