In the same way as there are "[full-stack](https://www.w3schools.com/whatis/whatis_fullstack.asp)" developers, I imagine myself as a "full-stack" learning designer. I am comfortable thinking about a project from orbit, as well as working "on the ground" with many different technology stacks (though I specialize in [only](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unity_(game_engine)) [a](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blender_(software)) [few](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drupal)). I offer two main skills for any project: Instructional Design and 3D Development.

---

## Instructional Design

I am trained in Instructional Design at the Masters level (M.Ed. University of Virginia 2015). I use the skills every workday in my [day job](https://news.virginia.edu/content/meet-makers-theyre-turning-ideas-reality-all-over-grounds), helping faculty imagine and realize novel and meaningful ways for learning in higher ed. 

In my freelance work I focus primarily on applying instructional design methods to immersive learning through extended reality (XR) technologies. These methods typically include performing needs assessments, writing design documents, crafting learning goals, and assessing how well the project achieves those goals. 

I both enjoy and believe in the model where the instructional designer is also the developer.

## 3D Development

I am a self-taught Unity developer (practicing since 2014). I use it frequently in my work, both for my day job, where I lead the Unity Community of Practice, and for freelance work. I also make games in my spare time. 

Due to my training as an educator, I would say my greatest strength as a coder is I write code that is intelligible and instructive to my future self / others. 

The affordances of 3D do not lend themselves to all content, but immersive learning is fundamentally about using the 3D "system" we know (that is, reality) and creating reality-adjacent activities that resonate with the learner's mind. In my collaborations I try to re-imagine learning from this perspective as opposed to re-creating non-immersive tasks in XR.
